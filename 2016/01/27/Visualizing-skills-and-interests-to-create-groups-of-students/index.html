How I Created the group

1. Categorize the data into 3 different levels.

The rate of each question was from 0 to 10, since no criteria provided to mark the level of each skill, I supposed it would be more objective to categorize the data into three rough levels: 1-4:introductory; 5-7:intermediate; 8-10:advanced.

The data was firstly been categoried, then clusterd to show the amount of each categorize.

2. Find classmates who are generally skilled at programming.

According to the data, we cound find that the number of our classmates who are skilled at programming was relatively small, firstly found out these classmates and put them into different groups.

Considered persons who had advanced skills in Information Visualization, Programming, Computer Graphics or Human-Computer Interaction were generally good at programming, found those people out!

In total 19 persons had advanced programming skills.

These persons were : 3, 4, 8, 25, 30, 32, 36, 39, 40, 42, 46, 48, 49, 59, 61, 62, 65, 72, 75.

3. Generate different groups :)

Randomly picked at least 2 persons that skilled at programming and 4 other persons to generate a group.
